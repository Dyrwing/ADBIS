Participation Process

How do I express interest in a study?

To express interest in a specific study, navigate to the project details page and click the "Express Interest" button. You'll be asked to complete a short form with your contact information and why you're interested in participating. The research team will then contact you to discuss next steps and determine if you meet the study criteria.

What happens after I submit my interest form?

After submitting your interest form, you can expect to be contacted by a member of the research team within 5-7 business days. They will provide more information about the study, answer any questions you may have, and schedule an initial screening to determine if you meet all eligibility criteria. Not everyone who expresses interest will qualify for a particular study.

Can I withdraw from a study after I've started?

Yes, participation in any research study is completely voluntary. You can withdraw at any time without providing a reason and without any penalty. If you decide to withdraw, simply inform the research team. Depending on the study, they may ask if you're willing to complete a brief exit interview or questionnaire, but this is also voluntary.

How much time commitment is required?

The time commitment varies greatly between studies. Some may involve a single survey that takes 30 minutes, while others might require weekly sessions over several months. The duration and frequency of participation is always clearly stated on the project details page. The research team will also discuss the time commitment in detail before you formally consent to participate.

Eligibility

What if I don't meet all the criteria for a study?

If you don't meet all criteria for a particular study, you unfortunately won't be able to participate in that specific project. However, we encourage you to browse other available studies as each has different eligibility requirements. You can also express general interest through our homepage, and we can notify you when studies matching your profile become available.

Do I need to have a psychiatric diagnosis to participate?

Not all studies require participants to have a psychiatric diagnosis. Some research projects are looking for individuals with specific mental health conditions, while others need participants without any diagnosis to serve as a comparison group. Some studies may focus on general population experiences related to mental well-being, stress, or daily functioning. Each project clearly states its specific requirements.

Can I participate if I'm currently taking medication?

This depends on the specific study. Some studies exclude participants on certain medications because they may affect the outcomes being measured, while others specifically focus on individuals taking particular treatments. In many observational studies, medication use is permitted. The eligibility criteria will specify any medication restrictions, and the research team will review your current medications during the screening process.

Privacy and Data

How is my personal information protected?

We take data protection very seriously. All research studies must comply with GDPR and ethical standards for research. Your personal information is stored securely and accessed only by authorized research team members. In most cases, your data will be de-identified (separated from your name and contact details) when used for analysis. Detailed information about data protection procedures is provided in the informed consent document for each study.

Will my participation be confidential?

Yes, your participation is confidential. The research team will not disclose your identity or participation to others outside the study team without your permission. When research results are published, they are presented as group data without identifying individual participants. The only exception to confidentiality would be if there is a concern about immediate risk of harm to yourself or others, which would be clearly explained in the consent process.

What happens to my data after the study ends?

The handling of data after a study ends varies according to the research protocol. Typically, de-identified data is retained for a specified period (often 5-10 years) to allow for verification of findings if needed. You'll be informed during the consent process about how long your data will be kept and whether it might be used for future research. In some cases, you may be asked if your data can be included in a database for future studies, but this is always optional.

Compensation

Are participants compensated for their time?

Many studies provide compensation for participants' time and effort, though not all do. Information about compensation is always clearly stated on the project details page. Compensation may take various forms, including direct payment, gift cards, prize draws, or reimbursement for travel expenses. The amount typically reflects the time commitment and nature of participation required.

When and how is compensation provided?

The timing and method of compensation varies by study. Some studies provide payment after each session, while others provide the full amount upon completion. For longer studies, compensation might be provided at specific milestones. The research team will explain exactly when and how you'll receive compensation before you begin. Common payment methods include bank transfers, mobile payment services, gift cards, or cash (for in-person studies).

What if I withdraw from a study early?

If you withdraw from a study before completing all requirements, you'll typically receive compensation for the portions you did complete. For example, if you completed 2 out of 4 sessions, you would receive payment for those 2 sessions. Each study has its own policy regarding partial compensation, which will be explained in the informed consent document. The goal is to ensure fair compensation even if full participation isn't possible.
